Spherical Harmonics

The idea of the spectral transform method is to represent fields (like wind speed and temperature) as a sum of so called spherical harmonic functions. The globe shows now one of these spherical harmonic functions. They are characterised by zonal wavenumber m and total wavenumber n. You can change m and n with the sliders above. If you don't see sliders above please try using a different browser! (Firefox, Chrome and Safari should work on desktop and mobile devices)

Truncation

The truncation is the highest zonal or total wavenumber that we use. To demonstrate the impact of truncation on a familiar field the globe shows now the orography (height above sea level). This orography was obtained from the spectral field used operationally in IFS. The slider above allows you to change the truncation of this spectral field.
